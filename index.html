Intel Sustainability Milestones

Milestone timeline

This founding moment began Intel's long-term commitment to innovation, setting the stage for sustainable technology development.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation.

This breakthrough made computing more efficient and opened new possibilities for smaller, lower-power devices.

1971

First Microprocessor

The 4004 becomes the world's first commercial microprocessor.

The x86 platform helped standardize performance and efficiency across PCs and servers.

1978

8086 Processor

Intel launches the 8086, defining the x86 architecture that powers modern computing.

This processor brought advanced multitasking and efficiency to personal computers.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 for a new era of 32-bit computing performance.

The company then invested in renewable energy, energy efficiency, and emissions reduction.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches its highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations.

Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, and Enabling priorities shape the next decade of progress.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches the RISE strategy and 2030 sustainability goals.

The plan focuses on sustainable operations, energy efficiency, and industry collaboration.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions for global operations.

This milestone drastically lowers emissions and advances Intel’s global climate goals.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide.

The event builds partnerships to accelerate sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit for suppliers and industry leaders.

RISE Strategy

Under its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy, Intel sets ambitious 2030 goals, including driving industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Commitment

In 2022, Intel pledged to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) by 2040. This commitment builds on decades of environmental initiatives and partnerships across the semiconductor industry.

Water & Waste

Intel conserves billions of gallons of water annually and collaborates with local communities to restore watersheds. Intel also upcycles and recycles materials to reduce waste and advance a circular economy.
